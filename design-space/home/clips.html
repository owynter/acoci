News archive — filter chips + alternating accent cards.

Source: Chicagoland Chamber Newsroom landing page.

Hero library — wave + colored band, two types.

Two types:

Type A — Anchor heroes (decorative). Tall colored band + wave bottom. The page’s content sits on top in a white card that straddles the boundary — same pattern as Clip 05. The hero itself only holds an optional breadcrumb. Use for structured detail pages (event, member profile, news article).

Type B — Content heroes. Same band + wave, but the hero holds eyebrow + page title + lede. Page content flows normally below. Use for landing pages (Members, News, Events index, About).

Color slots: teal-deep (Membership), sail (Advocacy), blue-mid (Events), Mission gradient (News/featured), aqua (Member directory), aqua-soft (About). All sampled from existing palette — no new tokens.

Wave behavior: Type A waves are filled white (the card sits over them). Type B waves are filled aqua-soft (the page background — content flows naturally beneath).

Chamber pitch night returns May 22, with $25,000 in catalytic funding on the table.

ACOCI’s Small Business Development Council brings back its annual capital readiness showcase — six member businesses, one panel of regional investors, and the kind of feedback you usually have to leave the island to get.

Damian Richardson

May 9, 2026 · 5 min read

Share

The Anguilla Chamber of Commerce & Industry will host the fourth annual Small Business Capital Readiness Pitch Night on Thursday, May 22 at the Hansa Bank conference centre. Six pre-screened ACOCI member businesses will present investment-ready pitches to a panel of regional capital providers, competing for a share of $25,000 in catalytic funding contributed by the chamber’s sponsors.

The funding is structured as zero-interest, milestone-tied capital rather than equity — the kind of bridge financing that’s notoriously hard to secure for small operators on the island. “Banks want collateral, equity investors want scale we don’t have yet,” says Damian Richardson, who chairs the Chamber’s SBDC working group. “Pitch Night is for the in-between — the businesses that have proven the model and just need runway to grow it.”

What’s changed this year

The 2026 cohort has been narrowed from twelve applicants to six, following two months of advisory sessions with the SBDC team. Three of the six are returning from previous cohorts — not as repeat applicants, but as businesses that previously won and have hit their stage-one milestones. They’ll pitch for stage-two extension funding alongside the new entrants.

The investor panel has also expanded. In addition to the chamber’s longstanding partners at Caribbean Commercial Bank and the Anguilla Development Board, this year’s panel includes representatives from the OECS Business Council and a discretionary observer from the regional Caribbean Development Bank. The full panel and judging criteria are published here.

Why it matters for ACOCI members

Pitch Night is one of the few moments in the chamber’s calendar where the gap between policy work and operator outcomes closes completely. The funding is real, the feedback is on-the-record, and the businesses pitching are the same operators who sit on ACOCI’s committees and respond to its consultations the rest of the year.

Members who aren’t pitching are encouraged to attend. The format includes a 30-minute networking reception before the pitches begin, and a debrief panel afterwards where the investors discuss what separated the strongest applications from the rest — the kind of inside read that’s usually only available to people already in the room.

How to attend

Registration is free for ACOCI members and $35 for non-members. The event runs 5:30–8:30 PM. Members can register through the chamber portal; non-members can register at the door pending capacity. If your business is interested in being considered for the 2027 cohort, the SBDC team will be taking expressions of interest at the post-event reception.

Clip 09

Triangle decoration — the brand graphic in heroes.

Source: ACOCI brand kit triangle SVGs (Triangles-01/02/03.svg) — the canonical graphic element with a fixed angle (~66° from horizontal). Same shape used in the Chicagoland Chamber heroes for inspiration, but here in the actual ACOCI form.

Mechanism: the SVGs are loaded as CSS mask-image, so the fill color is set via background-color in the stylesheet. That means we can use any palette token without editing the source SVGs — one set of files, infinite color combinations.

Three approaches shown on three different hero surfaces. Each approach preserves the brand angle by always using the actual SVG paths — only opacity, size, position, and rotation change.

Approach A: subtle white-on-color backdrop. Reads as texture, doesn’t compete with the title.

Approach B: stronger brand-color cluster, top-right. One large triangle bleeding off the corner, one smaller triangle rotated 180° for visual rhythm.

Approach C: uses the composite SVG (Triangles-01) which preserves the canonical two-triangle relationship from the brand kit, then adds a small sunset accent for a punch of CTA color used decoratively.
